# Udaipur Travel Guide
Content to be enriched from Wikivoyage.

## Overview
Udaipur, the "City of Lakes," is in southern Rajasthan. It was the historic capital of the Mewar kingdom and is known for its palaces, lakes, and havelis. The city sits around Lake Pichola and offers a mix of heritage, culture, and natural beauty.

## Best time to visit
The best time to visit Udaipur is October–March, when the weather is cool and dry. Summers (April–June) are hot; monsoon (July–September) brings rain and humidity. Winter is ideal for sightseeing and boat rides on the lakes.

## Getting around
Auto-rickshaws and taxis are common. Ride-hailing apps work in the city. Walking is feasible in the old city and around Lake Palace Road. Boats operate on Lake Pichola for lake tours and hotel transfers. Rent a scooter or car for day trips to Kumbhalgarh or Eklingji.

## Top attractions
City Palace: A large palace complex on the east bank of Lake Pichola, with museums, courtyards, and views. Allow 2–3 hours.
Lake Pichola: A scenic artificial lake with boat rides and views of the Lake Palace and City Palace.
Jagdish Temple: A prominent Hindu temple near City Palace, with intricate carvings.
Saheliyon ki Bari: A garden with fountains, lotus pools, and pavilions, built for royal women.
Bagore ki Haveli: A restored haveli with museums and evening cultural shows.
Monsoon Palace: A hilltop palace with panoramic views; best at sunset.

## Neighborhoods
Old City: Around City Palace and Lake Pichola; dense, historic, many guesthouses and restaurants.
Hanuman Ghat / Lal Ghat: Popular with tourists; lakeside cafes and hotels.
Fateh Sagar: North of Pichola; quieter, good for families and nature.
